# Business Insights & Recommendations

## Insight 1: Restaurant Revenue
**Finding:** Biryani Bros leads in revenue despite not having the highest order count,
driven by a high average order value (₹680).

**Recommendation:** Protect high-AOV restaurants from heavy discounting.
Promotions on these restaurants erode margin without necessarily driving volume.

---

## Insight 2: Cancellation Rate by City
**Finding:** Nagpur has a 33.33% cancellation rate — highest across all cities —
despite having lower order volumes than Mumbai and Pune.

**Recommendation:** Investigate delivery partner coverage and restaurant reliability
in Nagpur before expanding operations there. High cancellation in a low-volume
market is an early warning sign.

---

## Insight 3: Customer Lifetime Value
**Finding:** Rahul Sharma leads lifetime value at ₹1,390 driven by both order
frequency and consistent order size. However high AOV customers can outrank
high frequency customers in lifetime value.

**Recommendation:** Retention strategy should segment customers by frequency
AND AOV separately — not treat all loyal customers the same way.

---

## Insight 4: Peak Order Time Slots
**Finding:** Lunch slot (12–15hrs) dominates both order volume and revenue,
indicating a strong working-professional customer base.

**Recommendation:** Prioritize delivery partner availability and restaurant prep
capacity during 12–15hrs. Targeted promotions during dinner slot could balance
load and grow off-peak revenue.

---

## Insight 5: Delivery Partner Performance
**Finding:** Anil Bhosale (Pune) averages 45.25 mins delivery time — highest
in the network — despite Pune having zero cancellations.

**Recommendation:** Pune's cancellation issue is not delivery-speed related.
Anil's performance should be flagged independently — slow delivery damages
customer experience even when orders complete successfully.

---

## Insight 6: Customer Retention
**Finding:** 5 out of 15 customers (33%) are one-time buyers who never returned.
Mumbai and Nagpur each contribute 2 one-time customers.

**Recommendation:** Launch re-engagement campaigns targeting one-time customers
within 7 days of first order. This segment is cheapest to convert since
acquisition cost is already spent.

---

## Insight 7: Cuisine Type AOV
**Finding:** Japanese cuisine has the highest AOV but only 2 orders —
statistically unreliable. Indian cuisine leads in volume with 9 orders
and ranks 2nd in AOV.

**Recommendation:** Do not over-invest in Japanese cuisine expansion based
on AOV alone — validate with higher order volume first. Indian cuisine
is the safest revenue bet combining both volume and value.

---

| City | Revenue | Orders | Cancellation Rate | Avg Delivery Time |

| Mumbai | ₹4,900 | 12 | 8.33% | 37.27 mins |
| Pune | ₹3,460 | 8 | 0.00% | 39.25 mins |

**Finding:** Mumbai is the most reliable market. Pune shows zero cancellations
with growth potential. Nagpur is highest risk. Unknown segment generated ₹1,510
with highest AOV — a data quality issue that needs resolution as these could
be misclassified high-value customers.

**Recommendation:** Resolve missing customer city data immediately —
Unknown segment has the highest AOV (₹503) and losing visibility on these
customers is a business risk.
